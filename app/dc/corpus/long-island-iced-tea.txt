Long Island Iced Tea

History:
The usual origin story credits Robert 'Rosebud' Butt, who says he created it in 1972 at the Oak Beach Inn on Long Island during a contest to invent a drink using triple sec. A competing claim traces it to Prohibition-era Long Island, Tennessee, where a bootlegger allegedly built a mixed-spirit drink designed to look exactly like iced tea in the glass.

Recipe:
Use 1/2 oz of Vodka, 1/2 oz of Tequila, 1/2 oz of Light rum, 1/2 oz of Gin, 1 dash of Coca-Cola, and Twist of of Lemon peel. Mix all contents in a highball glass and sitr gently. Add dash of Coca-Cola for the coloring and garnish with lemon or lime twist.

Fun fact:
The drink contains no tea whatsoever. The colour comes entirely from a splash of cola, and the entire design goal was camouflage: it looks like the most innocent thing on the menu while containing four base spirits. If the Prohibition origin story is true, it is one of the only cocktails engineered primarily to deceive law enforcement.
