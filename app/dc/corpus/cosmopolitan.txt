Cosmopolitan

History:
The modern Cosmopolitan was refined in the late 1980s, with credit usually going to Toby Cecchini in New York, who rebuilt a rough Miami version using Absolut Citron, Cointreau and fresh lime. It was a working bartender's drink until Madonna was photographed with one at a Grammys party in 1996, after which it became the defining cocktail of a decade.

Recipe:
Add the 1 1/4 oz of Absolut Citron, 1/4 oz of Lime juice, 1/4 oz of Cointreau, and 1/4 cup of Cranberry juice into cocktail shaker filled with ice. Shake well and double strain into large cocktail glass. Garnish with lime wheel.

Fun fact:
Sex and the City made the Cosmo so ubiquitous that many bartenders grew to resent it, and by the mid-2000s some bars quietly stopped listing it out of sheer fatigue. Cecchini himself has said he finds his own most famous creation a bit embarrassing.
