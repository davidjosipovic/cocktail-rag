Mai Tai

History:
Victor 'Trader Vic' Bergeron claimed to have created the Mai Tai in 1944 at his Oakland restaurant, using a rare aged Jamaican rum. He served it to visiting Tahitian friends, one of whom declared 'Maita'i roa ae', Tahitian for 'out of this world'. Don the Beachcomber disputed the claim for decades, and the two tiki pioneers feuded over it publicly.

Recipe:
Shake the 1 oz of Light rum, 1/2 oz of Orgeat syrup, 1/2 oz of Triple sec, 1 1/2 oz of Sweet and sour, and 1 of Cherry with ice. Strain into glass. Garnish and serve with straw.

Fun fact:
The Mai Tai was so popular it drank its own key ingredient into extinction: demand exhausted the world's supply of the 17-year Jamaican rum Trader Vic originally used, forcing him to reformulate. The drink was a victim of its own success within about a decade.
