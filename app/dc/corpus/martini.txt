Martini

History:
The Martini's origin is contested between Martinez, California, where a gold miner allegedly demanded something special, and Martini di Arma di Taggia, a bartender at New York's Knickerbocker Hotel around 1912. Early versions were sweet and vermouth-heavy; the drink got progressively drier through the 20th century until, by the 1950s, some drinkers were essentially ordering cold gin and gesturing vaguely at a vermouth bottle.

Recipe:
Straight: Pour the 1 2/3 oz of Gin, 1/3 oz of Dry Vermouth, and 1 of Olive into mixing glass with ice cubes. Stir well. Strain in chilled martini cocktail glass. Squeeze oil from lemon peel onto the drink, or garnish with olive.

Fun fact:
James Bond's 'shaken, not stirred' is considered an error by most bartenders, since shaking bruises the gin and clouds the drink. Some fans argue Bond deliberately drinks it wrong to seem less sophisticated than he is, which is a generous reading of a man who orders the same thing in every country he visits.
