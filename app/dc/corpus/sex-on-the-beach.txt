Sex on the Beach

History:
The drink is generally traced to Florida in the 1980s, during a spring break sales contest run by a peach schnapps distributor. The bartender who won reportedly named it after the only two things his customers had come to Florida for. It spread from American beach bars to European resort towns within a decade, largely on the strength of the name alone.

Recipe:
Build the 1 oz of Vodka, 3/4 oz of Peach schnapps, Cranberry juice to taste, and Grapefruit juice to taste in a highball glass filled with ice. Garnish with orange slice.

Fun fact:
The name is such an effective sales tool that some bars and cruise lines print it on menus as 'S.O.T.B.' to avoid awkwardness, which has never once stopped anyone from ordering it loudly and by its full name.
