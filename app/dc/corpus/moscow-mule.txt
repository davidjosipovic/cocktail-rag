Moscow Mule

History:
The Moscow Mule was invented in 1941 in Los Angeles as a solution to three separate business failures colliding. John Martin owned Smirnoff vodka that nobody in America wanted, Jack Morgan had a ginger beer nobody was buying, and a third partner had inherited a stock of unsold copper mugs. They combined all three problems into one drink, and it worked.

Recipe:
Use 2 oz of Vodka, 2 oz of Lime juice, and 8 oz of Ginger ale. Combine vodka and ginger beer in a highball glass filled with ice. Add lime juice. Stir gently. Garnish.

Fun fact:
Martin marketed the drink by walking into bars with a Polaroid camera, photographing the bartender holding a Mule, and leaving one copy while keeping the other to show the next bar. It was essentially a viral marketing campaign built on physical photographs, decades before anyone had a word for it, and it single-handedly created the American vodka market.
