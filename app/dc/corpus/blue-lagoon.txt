Blue Lagoon

History:
The Blue Lagoon is generally credited to Andy MacElhone at Harry's New York Bar in Paris in the early 1960s, son of the bartender who ran the same bar decades earlier. It arrived just as blue curacao was becoming widely available and bartenders were discovering that customers would order almost anything if it was a colour that did not occur in nature.

Recipe:
Use 1 oz of Vodka, 1 oz of Blue Curacao, Lemonade to taste, and Cherry to taste. Pour vodka and curacao over ice in a highball glass. Fill with lemonade, top with the cherry, and serve.

Fun fact:
Blue curacao is just orange liqueur with dye in it. It is made from the dried peel of the laraha citrus from the island of Curacao, and tastes identical to the clear and orange versions of the same product. The blue exists purely because someone realised a bright unnatural colour sells better than a beige one.
