Manhattan

History:
Legend says the Manhattan was invented at New York's Manhattan Club in the 1870s at a banquet hosted by Lady Randolph Churchill, mother of Winston. The story is almost certainly false, since she was in England and heavily pregnant at the time. The likelier origin is an unnamed bartender named Black working near Houston Street, whose contribution to drinking history earned him no first name and no plaque.

Recipe:
Use 3/4 oz of Sweet Vermouth, 2 1/2 oz Blended of Bourbon, dash of Angostura bitters, 2 or 3 of Ice, 1 of Maraschino cherry, and 1 twist of of Orange peel. Stirred over ice, strained into a chilled glass, garnished, and served up.

Fun fact:
The Manhattan is one of the few classics that survived Prohibition by getting better: Canadian whisky smuggled across the border was smoother than the bathtub spirits everything else was made with, so the Manhattan quietly upgraded while the rest of the cocktail world got worse.
