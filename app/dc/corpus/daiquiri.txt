Daiquiri

History:
The Daiquiri is named after a beach and iron mine near Santiago de Cuba, where American engineer Jennings Cox is said to have improvised it around 1898 after running out of gin while entertaining guests. Rum, lime and sugar were what the island had in abundance. The drink reached the United States via a Navy medical officer who introduced it to a Washington club, and from there it spread through the officer class.

Recipe:
Pour the 1 1/2 oz of Light rum, Juice of 1/2 of Lime, and 1 tsp of Powdered sugar into shaker with ice cubes. Shake well. Strain in chilled cocktail glass.

Fun fact:
The Daiquiri got a wartime boost for bureaucratic reasons: whiskey was rationed in the 1940s United States, while the Good Neighbor policy kept Caribbean rum flowing freely. Americans drank Daiquiris in enormous numbers largely because rum was the only thing easily available, which is possibly the only time foreign policy has directly designed a cocktail menu.
