Negroni

History:
The Negroni was reportedly born in Florence around 1919, when Count Camillo Negroni asked his bartender at Caffe Casoni to toughen up his usual Americano by replacing the soda water with gin. The bartender, Fosco Scarselli, obliged, and swapped the customary lemon slice for an orange peel so the waitstaff could tell the Count's stronger order apart from everyone else's. The drink spread through Florentine aperitivo culture and never left.

Recipe:
Use 1 oz of Gin, 1 oz of Campari, and 1 oz of Sweet Vermouth. Stir into glass over ice, garnish and serve.

Fun fact:
The Count's family later insisted the real inventor was a different Negroni entirely: General Pascal Olivier de Negroni, a French cavalry officer. The two branches of the family have been quietly disputing credit for over a century, which makes the Negroni possibly the only cocktail with an ongoing succession crisis.
