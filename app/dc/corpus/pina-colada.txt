Pina Colada

History:
The Pina Colada was created in Puerto Rico, most often credited to bartender Ramon 'Monchito' Marrero at the Caribe Hilton in San Juan around 1954, who reportedly spent three months developing it. A rival claim comes from Ramon Portas Mingot at Barrachina in Old San Juan. Both bars still display plaques asserting they invented it, roughly a mile apart.

Recipe:
Use 3 oz of Light rum, 3 tblsp of Coconut milk, and 3 tblsp of Pineapple. Mix with crushed ice in blender until smooth. Pour into chilled glass, garnish and serve.

Fun fact:
The Pina Colada is the official drink of Puerto Rico by government proclamation, making it one of very few cocktails with formal legal status. It owes much of its existence to the invention of cream of coconut, which was developed by a Puerto Rican agricultural researcher trying to make coconut easier to cook with, not to get anyone drunk.
