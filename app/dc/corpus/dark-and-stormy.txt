Dark and Stormy

History:
The Dark and Stormy comes from Bermuda, where the Royal Naval Officers' Club began brewing ginger beer around 1900 and sailors mixed it with the local Gosling's Black Seal rum. The name reportedly came from an old sailor who said the drink's colour resembled a cloud only a fool or a dead man would sail under.

Recipe:
Use 5 cl of Dark Rum and 10 cl of Ginger Beer. In a highball glass filled with ice add 6cl dark rum and top with ginger beer. Garnish with lime wedge.

Fun fact:
Gosling's holds a registered trademark on the name, meaning a Dark 'n Stormy made with any other rum is technically a trademark violation. The company has actually sent legal letters over it, making this one of the only cocktails where using the wrong brand of rum is a legal matter rather than a matter of taste.
