Irish Coffee

History:
Irish Coffee was invented in the winter of 1943 at Foynes, a flying-boat terminal in County Limerick, by chef Joe Sheridan. A transatlantic flight turned back in bad weather and the frozen passengers needed warming, so Sheridan added whiskey to their coffee. Asked whether it was Brazilian coffee, he replied that it was Irish coffee, and named the drink by accident.

Recipe:
Use 1 1/2 oz of Irish whiskey, 8 oz of Coffee, 1 tsp of Sugar, and 1 tblsp of Whipped cream. Heat the coffee, whiskey and sugar; do not boil. Pour into glass and top with cream; serve hot.

Fun fact:
When the Buena Vista Cafe in San Francisco tried to import the drink in 1952, the cream kept sinking. The owner took the problem to the Irish government's dairy board, who determined the cream had to be aged exactly 48 hours and whipped to a precise consistency to float. Solving this cocktail required national-level agricultural research, and the Buena Vista now serves around 2,000 of them a day.
