Cuba Libre

History:
The Cuba Libre dates to around 1900 in Havana, shortly after the Spanish-American War, when Coca-Cola first arrived on the island alongside American troops. The story goes that a captain in the US Signal Corps mixed Bacardi rum with the new American soda, added lime, and toasted with the independence slogan 'Por Cuba libre'. The name stuck to the drink permanently.

Recipe:
Build the 2 oz of Light rum, Juice of 1/2 of Lime, and Coca-Cola to taste in a Collins glass filled with ice. Garnish with lime wedge.

Fun fact:
There is a strict bar convention that a rum and Coke becomes a Cuba Libre only when lime is added. Order the wrong one and you will get a small, unsolicited lecture. This is the only cocktail where a single wedge of citrus changes the drink's political name.
