Whiskey Sour

History:
The Whiskey Sour descends directly from naval practice. British sailors were issued spirits and citrus on long voyages, the citrus to prevent scurvy and the sugar and water to make the ration drinkable. That template, spirit plus citrus plus sugar, is the entire sour family. The drink appears in print in Jerry Thomas's 1862 bartender's guide, one of the first cocktail books ever published.

Recipe:
Shake with ice. Strain into chilled glass, garnish and serve. If served 'On the rocks', strain 2 oz of Blended whiskey, Juice of 1/2 of Lemon, 1/2 tsp of Powdered sugar, 1 of Cherry, and 1/2 slice of Lemon into old-fashioned glass filled with ice.

Fun fact:
The optional egg white in a Whiskey Sour is the source of a long-running bar argument, and the technique for it is called a 'dry shake', where you shake without ice first. Bartenders will genuinely debate this at length. The foam serves no flavour purpose at all: it is purely texture and appearance, meaning the most contentious step in the recipe is essentially decorative.
