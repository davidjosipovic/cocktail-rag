Amaretto Sour

History:
The Amaretto Sour rose with the American cocktail boom of the 1970s, when Disaronno pushed hard into the United States market and the sour template was applied to nearly every liqueur available. For decades it was dismissed as a cloying novelty made with bottled mix, until bartender Jeffrey Morgenthaler published a rebuilt version in 2012 using fresh lemon, egg white and a slug of bourbon, which rehabilitated the drink almost overnight.

Recipe:
Use 1 1/2 oz of Amaretto and 3 oz of Sour mix. Shake and strain. Garnish with a cherry and an orange slice.

Fun fact:
Amaretto tastes overwhelmingly of almond but usually contains no almonds at all. The flavour typically comes from apricot or peach kernels, which share the same aromatic compound. This means the most almond-flavoured drink on most menus is frequently safe for people with nut allergies, and the most nut-free-sounding ones sometimes are not.
