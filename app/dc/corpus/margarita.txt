Margarita

History:
Nobody can agree on who invented the Margarita, and at least half a dozen people have claimed it. One popular story credits Carlos 'Danny' Herrera, who supposedly built it in Tijuana in the late 1930s for a showgirl who was allergic to every spirit except tequila. Another credits Dallas socialite Margarita Sames, who claimed she mixed it at her Acapulco holiday home in 1948. The drink is essentially a tequila Daisy, and 'margarita' is Spanish for 'daisy', which suggests the truth is duller than any of the stories.

Recipe:
Rub the rim of the glass with the lime slice to make the salt stick to it. Take care to moisten only the outer rim and sprinkle the salt on it. The salt should present to the lips of the imbiber and never mix into the cocktail. Shake the other 1 1/2 oz of Tequila, 1/2 oz of Triple sec, 1 oz of Lime juice, and Salt to taste with ice, then carefully pour into the glass.

Fun fact:
The world's largest Margarita held over 8,500 gallons and required a purpose-built tank, a crane, and presumably a very patient health inspector.
